Everyday Antifascism Tactics
A diversity of tactics is key to opposing far-right oppression. For those unwilling or unable to directly confront fascist action in person, there are still a variety of steps to take that can have a positive effect towards defending communities and targeted groups of people.
Research
Fascism is not an ideology that suddenly springs fully-formed upon the scene, but is slowly and carefully cultivated over time. Political pundits might scoff at the notion that nascent far-right movements could have any effect on larger politics, but the evidence is there that politicians will seize upon fascistic policies as a means to remain in power. So become familiar with far-right groups operating in your town and their connections to larger organizations nationwide. Pay attention to the speeches and press releases given by the politicians they support. Learn about them online, in person, or via infiltration—just be careful!
Communicate
Chances are there are people in your community that are as alarmed and frightened by far-right politics as you are, or perhaps even more. Therefore, it’s essential to get the word out that they are not alone and to warn others of the potentially dangerous nature of far-right influence. Producing communicative materials like flyers, stickers, leaflets, banners, videos, songs, art, and books is an effective way to build networks and help bolster mutual support for one another. Clear communication will help your friends and neighbors find common ground and stand up for one another.
Dox
Some say “sunshine is the best disinfectant”, and this tactic exemplifies that notion. Many far-right activists and sympathizers who hold oppressive, repugnant, and even violent beliefs don’t want those beliefs being widely known. Therefore, it’s important to make those private views public and connect them explicitly with individuals who hold them. This can include notifying their employers, teachers, neighbors, and wider communities about what is brewing in their midst. The goal is to expose far-right activists and organizers until they are disowned, fired, ostracized, and shut down—to make the social costs of organizing around such views so high that there is no choice but to quit.
Support
The typical targets of far-right politics include marginalized groups. These can be immigrants, the poor, women (especially in the realm of reproductive rights), and more. Show your support for these groups by working with them to help achieve their goals and defend their rights. Oppose police brutality in all its forms. Building community and solidarity with people who are targeted by far-right activists and organizers strengthens and protects everyone involved.
Educate
Vigilance against far-right oppression requires continuous self-improvement and education. Organizing reading groups, concerts, shows, art exhibitions, political training, athletics, fundraising, and other events to help educate your community will bring people together and make them smarter about working together to defend themselves. One of the primary tactics of far-right activism is disinformation—only a well-educated, critical opposition can recognize their deception and successfully counter its influence. Sharing ideas and sharpening intellectual acuity will help you and your community a great deal in the struggle against violence and oppression.
